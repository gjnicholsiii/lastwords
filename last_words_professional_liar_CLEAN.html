welcome to my post-mortem monologue. I'd stand, but you know... circumstances.

To those who think they knew me, you saw what I allowed you to see. Watching shadows on water and thinking you've touched the depths. I performed normalcy with such grace, didn't I? Wore my skin like a perfectly tailored suit. You never noticed how my laughter changed its tune, each version of me singing a different song.

To the ones who called me friend, half of you were right. The other half? I transformed your betrayals into verses so beautiful you'd weep reading them, without realizing you were crying for your own sins.

Friend is doing heroic work in that sentence.

To my lovers, I kept something from each of you, little trophies of destruction. A truth about intimacy, it leaves traces that never wash away. Some nights I'd arrange your memories, wondering at how easily I gave myself or how I quietly allowed you to walk away.

To one who wrote me a monster to paint over their sins. You did well. I almost admire how perfectly you destroyed what wasn't yours to break. How many men helped you perfect that portrait? But darling, the bloated corpse of truth won’t stay buried. They float. And the water's rising...

To my family, I loved you in ways that felt like drowning. Your expectations were weights, pretty shackles, obligatory jewelry. But of my own forging. Sorry I could never quite be the masterpiece I wanted to hang on your wall. I was always more of a crime scene. Beautiful only to those who understand my violence.

*laughs from the coffin*

To mine. In my perfect performances and elegant deceptions, you were the only real thing. Not a role I played or a mask I wore. Just pure, unscripted love. While others demanded their pound of flesh, you just wanted stories and hugs, Your laughter just joy in its purest form. When the world was drowning in beautiful lies, you were my lifeline to what mattered. Even in a liar's house, you kept me honest

Oh, and to those sitting in the back, pretending to mourn. I watched you practicing your sad faces in mirrors. I wrote your names in my memory, not for revenge, but because sometimes the best revenge is letting people know they were never important enough to hate.

The back row has suddenly become interested in the carpet.

To the ones who truly loved me, I'm sorry I was a vault with a welcome mat. Sorry I made you solve riddles just to reach my heart. But god, wasn't it beautiful when you did? When you found me behind all my walls and said "Oh, there you are. I've been looking for you."

And to myself, the self I never quite became. We had plans, didn't we? Wild, terrifying plans. Some of them we achieved in secret. Others we buried so deep, they're still growing like nightshade between my ribs.

To those who feared me. Your instincts were better than your courage. You sensed the predator beneath my smile, didn't you? But convinced yourselves it was just imagination. Funny how prey can smell danger but still freeze when it wears a familiar face.

To those who worshipped me. You beautiful, desperate fools. You built altars to a mirage, prayed to a carefully crafted illusion. Though I'll admit, sometimes your devotion made me want to become the god you thought I was.

Please remain seated during the deification.

Almost.

To those who tried to fix me. Thank you for the entertainment. Watching you try to solve me like a puzzle missing half its pieces was delicious. Did you ever realize you were using your own broken parts to try to mend mine?

To my greatest betrayer, blood of my blood. Monsters dwell not beneath beds, but beneath familiar roofs. You thought yourself my undoing, yet in your wake I arose everything you feared you could never become. And that victory etches a smile even death cannot claim.

And to all my mirrors. Want to know what I really saw? A carnival of everyone I could have been, dancing behind my eyes. Some nights I'd catch my reflection wearing someone else's smile. Other nights, I wasn't there at all. Just an elegant arrangement of masks performing a person.

And oh, the secrets.

*laughs darkly*

Some are buried so deep they'll birth new religions when they're found. Others still walk among you, wearing the faces of people you trust. A few wait in sealed envelopes, timed to arrive after I'm gone.

Consider them love letters from the beyond.

The many versions of me? There's one in each of your minds, performing different roles in different plays. To some, I was saint, to others sinner. Mentor to these, monster to those. Lover, fighter, friend, fiend.

At last, a version everyone can misremember equally.

All true.

All false.

All me.

We're all just stories in the end, and I made sure to be a different genre for everyone.

*straightens tie in death*

Final thoughts? Life's a dark comedy, death's the punchline, and this funeral is my last performance. I hope you're all thoroughly entertained. Or disturbed. Or both.

*adjusts death shroud*

So here I am, or rather, here I was. A walking contradiction, a living library of lies and truths so intertwined they became poetry.

I lived as a question mark and died as an ellipsis...

Don't cry too hard. Or do. Just know I'm critiquing your performance from whatever comes next.

*final bow*
